You Have the Power to Change Your Story

Dear Tamson, this guide is a starting point for a journey towards better health. Pre-diabetes is a warning sign, but it's also an opportunity. With small, consistent changes to your lifestyle, you can prevent or delay type 2 diabetes and feel more vibrant and energetic than ever. You can do this!

Your 4-Step Wellness Plan

1. Nourish Your Body

Focus on whole foods: vegetables, fruits, lean proteins, and whole grains. Try the "plate method": fill half your plate with non-starchy veggies, a quarter with lean protein, and a quarter with high-fiber carbs. Limit sugary drinks and processed snacks.

2. Get Moving

Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate exercise per week. A brisk walk, a bike ride, or a dance class are all great options. Find activities you enjoy! This helps your body use insulin more effectively and can help with weight management.

3. Manage Your Weight

Losing even a small amount of weight (5-7% of your body weight) can make a huge difference in preventing type 2 diabetes. Focus on sustainable habits rather than quick fixes. Every healthy choice is a victory.

4. Rest & De-stress

Poor sleep and high stress can raise blood sugar levels. Aim for 7-8 hours of quality sleep per night. Practice stress-reducing activities like meditation, deep breathing, yoga, or spending time in nature. Your mental health is key to your physical health.

Sample Day of Eating

Breakfast

Oatmeal with berries, nuts, and a sprinkle of cinnamon. Or scrambled eggs with spinach and a slice of whole-wheat toast.

Lunch

A large salad with grilled chicken or chickpeas, lots of colorful veggies, and a light vinaigrette dressing.

Dinner

Baked salmon with a side of roasted broccoli and a small portion of quinoa or brown rice.

Snack Idea: An apple with peanut butter, a handful of almonds, or Greek yogurt.
